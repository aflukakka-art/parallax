Introduction

Quantum mechanics is the branch of physics that explains how particles like electrons and photons behave on atomic and subatomic scales. It challenges classical physics with concepts of wave-particle duality and probability.

Uncertainty Principle

Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle states that it’s impossible to simultaneously determine both the exact position and momentum of a particle. Mathematically represented as:

Δx · Δp ≥ ħ / 2

This principle reveals the fundamental limits of measurement and the probabilistic nature of the quantum world.

Quantum Models

Quantum models such as the Schrödinger model describe electrons as wave functions instead of fixed orbits. These models help predict energy levels, probability densities, and quantum tunneling effects that shape our understanding of matter.
